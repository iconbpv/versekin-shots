How deep does the inner ring go?

The shipped ring shows two axes — familiarization outside, internalization inside. This explores how many levels internalization really has, whether to split it, and how an honest missed-recall should read.

The levels of internalization

From the #211 model. Five tiers exist, but the inner ring only lives above the memorized line — so it has three fillable levels, one mirror of each familiarization stage outside it.

TierNameEarned byRing
I0Learningnot yet recalled from a blank pageouter only
I1Memorizedfamiliarization complete — the two axes meet hereinner = empty
I2Retained2 clean first-try reviews (no retry)seg 1
I3Masteredreached the top 60-day interval, streak intactseg 2
I4Engravedtop interval and a clean first-try streak ≥ 5seg 3
The hinge for a third ring: I2/I3/I4 are each gated by two independent signals — a clean first-try streak (accuracy) and the spaced-review interval reached (durability). They diverge exactly on the honest dimension: you can keep every review on schedule via retries yet rarely nail it first-try. That divergence is what a third ring can make visible.

Option A

Two rings — inner split into 3 tiers

The straight answer to your first question. Inner ring becomes three segments (I2·I3·I4), a clean mirror of the three outer stages. One internalization axis, one meeting point.

Option B · recommended

Three rings — internalization split into Retention + Durability

Decomposes internalization into its two real inputs. Middle = Retention (clean first-try streak, toward 5). Inner = Durability (how far the review interval has stretched, 1 day → 60). All three full = engraved.

A compromise. Outer familiarization, inner durability arc, and the retention tier collapsed into a single centre gem — bronze (Retained) · silver (Mastered) · gold (Engraved).

Your honest-regression case

“At level 2, a missed first-try should affect the ring.” Here is the exact state — stage 3, ri 5, streak reset to 0 — drawn three ways. This is where Option B earns its extra ring.

In A and C a missed first-try dims the whole internalization inner ring / core back toward Memorized — truthful, but it reads as “you lost it.” In B only the Retention ring empties; the Durability ring stays nearly full — “you’ve kept it on schedule; your first-try streak just reset.” That’s the M5 framing #211 insists on: dormant, not lost. The earned peak (itierPeak) and any badge never regress regardless.

Recommendation

Go with B, rendered at two sizes. The three-ring split is the only option that answers all three of your questions at once — it has the three levels, it segments them, and the missed-first-try lands on the right ring without reading as loss.

The one real cost is inline legibility. Resolve it the way the app already handles the book map: a compact form in the verse row (outer + a single combined internalization arc, ~26px) that expands to the full three-ring detail on tap / in Review / on Progress — where there’s room for the Retention and Durability rings to be read separately. Same data, two densities.

What building it takes

All specified in #211 — the ring is the display end of the real itier model, which the shipped ri-proxy can’t express (it has no notion of a clean first-try streak).

  1. Per-verse state — add itier, itierPeak (never decreases), cleanStreak. Migration derives them from existing stage/ri on load.
  2. First-try capture — session.js records the accuracy of the first recall attempt before any “try again”; only that advances or resets cleanStreak.
  3. Pure tier functions in engine.js (testable core) computing I0–I4 from {stage, cleanStreak, ri}.
  4. Ring renderer — replace the single inner arc with Retention + Durability; compact vs. detail sizes.
  5. Sync — the new fields ride the existing per-verse last-write-wins; no new merge rule.
