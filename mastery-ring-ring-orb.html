Ranking up the long haul.

The inner ring holds the first six review rungs (1 → 30 days) — a segment deepens as you hold it, fades if you slip. Past 30 days a centre orb takes over and ranks up: warm gold at 60 days, climbing through gem tiers to a diamond capstone at a full year. Borrowed straight from ranked-tier systems.

What we borrowed from ranked tiers

Principles taken
  • Distinct colour per tier — a rank is legible at a glance, never a subtle shade-shift
  • Warm metal → cool gem escalation — gold is the entry floor; the jewels visibly outrank it
  • The peak gets special treatment — diamond alone earns a sparkle + a warm gold aura (the “Challenger” moment)
  • Shape can escalate too — the capstone is a sparkle, not just a colour, so the top reads even without hue
Deliberately left out
  • No competition. No leaderboards, no divisions, no “demotion” language — the app is intrinsic-first (P6/P9). This is a private, factual record of how deep a verse is held
  • A slip fades a rung, it doesn’t “derank” you with a red warning
  • The earned peak and any badge never regress — the rank shows current durability, the badge shows what you reached

A missed recall

The held rung fades to a paler tint; in the orb phase, the rank steps down one gem. Never framed as a loss.

On the inner ring, the rung you couldn’t hold fades lighter and you drop one — “re-earn this one,” never “you lost the verse” (M5: dormant, not lost). In the orb phase, a miss steps the rank back one gem (amethyst → garnet) and drops you toward the ring. Peak and badges never regress.
